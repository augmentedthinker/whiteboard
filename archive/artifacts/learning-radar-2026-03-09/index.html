Today’s Artifact · Learning Radar

Learning Radar · 2026-03-09

This page turns your instinct into a working model: one system for curating the information stream, and another for identifying the next capabilities just outside your current edge — both as an individual and as a human+agent team.

Core idea

Two loops, not one

  • Loop 1 — Curation: what information is worth tracking on an ongoing basis?
  • Loop 2 — Horizon mapping: what capabilities are close enough to matter, but not yet fully owned?
  • Without curation, you drown in signal.
  • Without horizon mapping, you learn randomly instead of strategically.
The point is not to know everything. The point is to keep your attention aligned with the capabilities you actually want to grow.
Working thesis

What this should feel like in practice

  • A small number of domains you monitor consistently
  • A repeatable way to compress noise into usable signal
  • A visible map of what you already know, what the team can already do, and what is one step beyond
  • A bias toward learning that expands real-world output capacity
Loop 1

The Information Curation Stack

Instead of one giant bucket called “things I should follow,” break the stream into lanes with different cadences and purposes.

Tier A — High-frequency signal

Check often. Mostly AI labs, major releases, capability jumps, key platform/tool changes, and highly relevant build trends.

Tier B — Medium-frequency relevance

Check weekly. Product strategy, interface trends, agent workflows, indie builder tactics, robotics, automation, personal systems.

Tier C — Slow-burn depth

Check more selectively. Philosophy, cognition, health systems, long-form essays, serious books, durable frameworks.

Tier D — Opportunistic curiosity

Interesting but non-core. Keep this from colonizing the rest of the system.

Design principle

Don’t curate “topics.” Curate by job-to-be-done.

  • What helps me build better?
  • What helps me see where the field is going?
  • What helps me make better decisions?
  • What helps me become more capable as a thinker/operator?

That framing keeps the system attached to your actual life instead of turning into passive content consumption.

Loop 2

The Next-Horizon Map

The second system is about capability edges. Not abstract curiosity — nearby powers that are reachable with focused learning and would materially expand what you and the team can do.

Four horizon buckets

  • Owned: already understood / already operational
  • Usable: can do it, but inconsistently or with friction
  • Adjacent: right outside the current edge; likely best ROI for learning
  • Distant: exciting, but premature for current focus
Critical distinction

Separate conceptual capability from operational capability

  • You, conceptually: what you understand well enough to reason about
  • You, personally: what you can execute yourself
  • The team: what becomes possible when you combine your judgment with agent execution

That distinction matters because some horizons are knowledge gaps, some are workflow gaps, and some are tooling gaps.

Example board

What you may already own

  • High-level understanding of where AI/agents are going
  • Taste for useful interfaces and coherent systems
  • Ability to shape, critique, and direct technical work
  • Ability to generate strong exploratory concepts and product directions
  • Early practical fluency in using agents as collaborators
Example board

What feels adjacent / high ROI

  • Reliable information-briefing pipelines
  • Daily output packaging into public-facing updates
  • Review-first publishing systems
  • More robust browser / interface control workflows
  • A stronger framework for recurring learning and capability reviews
Question 1

What should we keep up with?

Only information streams that either sharpen your judgment, expand your capability, or create opportunity.

Question 2

What are we almost able to do?

Those nearby capabilities are usually the best places to invest learning effort.

Question 3

What learning actually changes output?

Prioritize knowledge that creates new kinds of work, not just new things to think about.

Operating ritual

A lightweight weekly process

1. Review the signal lanes

What happened in AI, agents, product, and other chosen domains that actually matters?

2. Distill the useful parts

Capture only the insights, tools, shifts, or opportunities worth remembering.

3. Update the horizon map

What moved from adjacent to usable? What new adjacent skills appeared?

4. Choose one learning target

Pick the single most leverage-rich thing to explore next.

The win condition is not “stay informed.” The win condition is “continuously become more capable in the directions that matter.”
