Synthesis of the Aetherium Shard

Transcribed from the Lost Folios of Master Ignis, circa 14th Era.

The pursuit of the Aetherium Shard requires absolute precision. One does not simply mix the reagents; one must align the crystalline structures along the axis of the ambient energy field. If the alignment is off by even a fraction of a degree, the resulting resonance will shatter the containment vessel.

The Reagents

Begin with pulverized obsidian, purified under the light of a waxing moon. Slowly fold in the luminescent spores gathered from the deep caverns of the northern ridge. The mixture must be heated in a crucible forged of pure aurum, never exceeding the temperature of a dying ember. It is in this delicate thermal window that the true transmutation begins, binding the physical dust to the ethereal hum of the void.

The Invocation

Once the crucible sings with a low, resonant frequency, the catalyst must be introduced. This is not an act of force, but of persuasion. Speak the syllables of the ancients, letting the vibration of your voice guide the atoms into their new, eternal lattice. The shard will form suddenly, a flash of blinding azure light signaling the birth of a star contained within glass.
