Trajectory-Oriented Operating List

Deep To-Do

A broader operating list than the Next Steps page: grounded in memory, but willing to infer where Christopher is actually headed. This is less a shopping list and more a map of what should happen if the current trajectory is taken seriously.

How to read this page

The existing Next Steps page is the concise front-stage action shelf. This page goes deeper. It includes not only the obvious next actions, but also the infrastructure, clarifications, sequencing, and medium-term moves that seem necessary if your current work is going to become more coherent, profitable, and real.

Some items below are direct carry-forwards from memory. Others are inferred from the direction those memories imply. The question behind this page is simple:

If we take your actual trajectory seriously, what work should exist around it?

Priority stack

If I had to compress the whole page into a top-order sequence, it would be:

  • 1. Stabilize the operating environment — storage pressure, troubleshooting clarity, and infrastructure confidence.
  • 2. Build a real output rhythm — especially proof-of-work publishing and structured AI learning distillation.
  • 3. Advance one monetizable lane — most likely citation workflow / legal-tool direction before broader empire-building.
  • 4. Keep Balance alive, but demand consequence from it — it should shape life, not merely symbolize it.
  • 5. Protect the human substrate — health, relationships, rhythm, recovery, and actual aliveness.

The actual to-do list

Venture / Applied AI

Build a repeatable AI learning pipeline

  • Choose the core channels/sources that genuinely matter instead of letting AI media sprawl across all available attention.
  • Define a weekly cadence for intake: when you watch/read, how long, and what counts as enough.
  • Create a distillation habit: convert long-form inputs into short mirrored notes, bullet summaries, or public-ready takes.
  • Decide which distilled outputs should stay private, which should feed X, and which deserve expansion into Blogger essays.

Turn publishing into a system, not a mood

  • Refine the social-media strategy into a lightweight publishing grammar: what kinds of posts exist, what tone they take, and how often they appear.
  • Establish a reliable end-of-day or end-of-session packaging rhythm for proof-of-work.
  • Keep X manual and honest, but reduce friction by preparing better ready-to-review draft packages.
  • Deepen the Blogger lane around lived OpenClaw field notes, not generic AI discourse.

Pick one first-dollar lane and move it forward on purpose

  • Treat the citation/legal workflow as the most legible first-revenue candidate unless a stronger near-term offer emerges.
  • Clarify whether the near-term offer is a product, a service-assisted workflow, or a hybrid.
  • Define what “good enough to show a real person” means without overstating capability.
  • Create a small, concrete client-facing narrative: who it helps, what pain it removes, what level of trust it can honestly claim.

Advance backend capability where it actually matters

  • Explore Vercel or similar backend infrastructure for stateful systems, server-side secrets, and cross-device continuity.
  • Use that not just for the legal app, but for future systems like Next Steps completion tracking or agent accountability loops.
  • Decide which projects still belong on GitHub Pages and which are now outgrowing static hosting.
Remote Viewer / System Architecture

Keep Remote Viewer curated and operationally honest

  • Continue protecting the main menu from clutter.
  • Use Today’s Artifacts as the living working shelf rather than a sentimental archive.
  • Promote only what is genuinely active; archive or remove stale conceptual debris.
  • Keep signature pages and provenance discipline strong so the system stays legible over time.

Decide what Remote Viewer is becoming

  • Clarify whether it is primarily a launcher, a living artifact shelf, a control surface, or a browser-native continuity operating system.
  • Let that decision shape future page additions instead of growing by accumulation alone.
  • Use the Troubleshooting page, Styles lane, and Today curation as early evidence of a more structured ecosystem.

Strengthen reusable architecture

  • Keep moving universal UI into shared assets where it actually reduces maintenance risk.
  • Be careful with broad replacement passes that can damage handcrafted pages.
  • Smoke-test key surfaces after shared changes: main menu, Today, Tools, Styles, and any heavily customized artifacts.
Troubleshooting / Reliability

Reduce ambient technical uncertainty

  • Investigate the degraded memory_search path so smart retrieval works as well as raw file access.
  • Continue documenting cron behavior separately by creation path, execution path, and delivery path.
  • Track the browser relay problem as a tool-handoff issue, not merely a browser-extension issue.
  • Keep clarifying whether Chromebook friction is a storage problem, a Crostini startup problem, or a broader session-state problem.

Make troubleshooting more durable

  • Use the Troubleshooting page for active recurring issues.
  • When an issue becomes deep enough, write a formal incident report so future-you does not have to rediscover the diagnosis.
  • Separate symptoms, evidence, theories, and confirmed fixes more explicitly.
Connection / Relational Identity

Prevent the frontier project from swallowing ordinary human life

  • Actually schedule one low-friction hangout rather than keeping friendship as a background ideal.
  • Keep public-facing identity honest: proof-of-work over performance, signal over cosplay.
  • Notice which parts of relational life are being postponed by fascination with tools, systems, and future-building.

Give spiritual life real form

  • Clarify what “spiritual journey” means in practice right now rather than as an abstract noble phrase.
  • Choose one repeatable form: reading, prayer, contemplation, community, ritual, journaling, or something adjacent.
  • See whether this lane belongs partly under Connection because it changes how you relate, not just what you believe.
Life Stewardship

Handle real-world maintenance before it becomes psychic drag

  • Schedule the doctor appointment.
  • Schedule the dentist cleaning.
  • Reduce storage pressure on the ChromeOS/shared side before it becomes a recurring destabilizer.
  • Keep the two-Chromebook architecture intentional rather than accidental: one as primary human-facing machine, one as more agent-dedicated environment.

Make continuity maintenance routine

  • Keep using the three-lane continuity push before resets, updates, or reboots.
  • Periodically review memory lanes and promote durable lessons into long-term memory.
  • Continue using long-form session reports when a day meaningfully changes system direction.
Adventure / Aliveness

Refuse the life of endless optimization

  • Pick one genuinely new experience and actually do it.
  • Do not let “interesting future work” become a substitute for present-tense vividness.
  • Preserve some lane in the system for beauty, warmth, play, and emotional restoration — not all valuable artifacts need to be strategic.

Protect the sense of being alive inside the build

  • Keep making room for artifacts or experiences that regulate mood and recover spirit, not only sharpen strategy.
  • Let Balance measure aliveness honestly rather than turning it into another moral performance metric.

What feels most strategic over the next 2–4 weeks

  • Clarify the first-dollar lane and make one small external-facing move that tests it.
  • Create the structured AI learning/publishing rhythm so AI intake turns into output instead of private overwhelm.
  • Clean and stabilize the environment enough that infrastructure anxiety drops.
  • Keep Remote Viewer curated so it remains a truthful mirror rather than a beautiful junk drawer.
  • Pick one action from Connection and one from Stewardship so the system does not become all frontier and no life.

Bottom line

The deeper to-do is not merely “do more things.” It is: stabilize the environment, turn learning into output, turn capability into one real offer, and keep the human life underneath it from becoming collateral damage.
