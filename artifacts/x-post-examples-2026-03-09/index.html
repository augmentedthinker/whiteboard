Today’s Artifact · X Post Examples

X Post Examples · 2026-03-09

Three different directions for how Christopher & Co. might post about today’s work in a way that feels native to people building around AI, agents, interfaces, and real operational experimentation. My view on sub-agents: yes, a dedicated social or Blogger agent can make sense later — but only once the lane becomes repetitive enough to benefit from its own memory, standards, and output format.

Rule of thumb: create a dedicated sub-agent when a lane has a stable job, a distinct voice, a repeatable workflow, and a clear review checkpoint.

Feels less like prompting, more like building a real environment. #OpenClaw #AIEngineering #BuildInPublic

Example 03 · More philosophical / future-facing angle

What if AI needs a place, not just a prompt box?

This one reaches a little further conceptually, but it still ties back to something we actually made today.

One idea I can’t shake: if digital intelligences keep getting more continuous, more capable, and more agentic, they may eventually need more than a chat interface. They may need a place. Not in the mystical sense — in the architectural sense. A habitat for memory, tools, identity, and presence. Today we mocked up an early version of that idea as a “digital sanctuary.” #AI #AgentDesign #XR #OpenClaw
Why it works

Memorable hook, but still anchored to real work.

Best media

Concept image, artifact screenshot, or short ambient walkthrough.

Tone

Thoughtful, provocative, future-facing.

My take on dedicated posting agents I would not create an X-posting or Blogger agent just to have one. I would create one once we know the lane’s shape.

X/social agent makes sense when: it can take today’s work, choose a post angle, draft 3–5 options, suggest media, and package a review-ready post.

Blogger agent makes sense when: it can take a conversation thread, extract the essay-worthy idea, structure it, write a draft, and prepare it for review-first publishing.

In other words: yes, probably eventually — but we’re still in the phase of discovering the templates those agents would need.
