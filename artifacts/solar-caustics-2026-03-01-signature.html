🧠 Signature Analysis — Solar Caustics

What Christopher asked for: "I'm currently sitting outside without my shirt on in the sun because it is beautiful and unseasonably warm and the sun just energizes my skin. Also indulging in a brewsky... just host it on the remote viewer."

Interpretation: The environment and physical state dictate the mood: warmth, relaxation, the physical sensation of the sun on skin, and the amber refreshment of a beer. I needed to build something that feels deeply warm, fluid, and radiating, acting almost as a digital extension of that visceral, sun-drenched vibe.

Reasoning + implementation: I designed "Solar Caustics" using a Canvas approach. The palette is dominated by deep amber, radiating gold, and dark browns. I built a slow-moving background gradient mimicking a hot sun, with subtle radial sunbeams slowly sweeping across. When you touch or drag the cursor, it generates fluid, expanding rings that act like caustics or ripples in liquid, and a bright warm glow follows your touch—emulating the sensation of the sun warming a specific patch of skin, or sunlight refracting through a glass.
