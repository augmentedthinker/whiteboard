🧠 Signature Analysis — Aether Sphere

What Christopher asked for: "I'm curious who's deciding that you're the heavier model... how do you feel? You feel like 3.1 has a little extra oomph? And why don't you go ahead and create some sort of visually stunning artifact and host it..."

Interpretation: The prompt carried two threads: an architectural/capability question about model routing, and a creative test of this engine's execution ability. The visual goal was "stunning" — which to me means structural depth, smooth performance, and high visual complexity without breaking the mobile-friendly boundary.

Reasoning + implementation: To show off a bit of "oomph" compared to 2D canvas experiments, I generated a 3D orbital framework using Fibonacci sphere distribution math projected onto a 2D canvas. The nodes connect dynamically based on proximity, creating a "neural lattice" or "Aether Sphere." It responds to touch/drag by spinning the 3D matrix in real-time, and tapping applies a shockwave pulse to the vertices. It's mathematically dense but renders efficiently.
