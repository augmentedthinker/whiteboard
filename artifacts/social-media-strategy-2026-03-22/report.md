# Social Media Strategy

## Purpose
Social media is treated as a **tool for signal, documentation, and learning** — not identity validation or emotional regulation.

---

## Core Philosophy

- Social media is **not identity**
- Social media is **not validation**
- Social media is:
  - A **public notebook**
  - A **signal channel**
  - A **learning interface**

---

## Functional Breakdown

### 1. Input Stream (Learning)
- Follow relevant AI/tech voices
- Stay informed on trends and ideas
- Avoid passive overconsumption

### 2. Archive (Documentation)
- Record ideas, builds, experiments
- Create a visible breadcrumb trail
- Demonstrate continuity over time

### 3. Feedback (Experimentation)
- Analyze response to posts
- Treat engagement as **data, not judgment**
- Run experiments on:
  - Framing
  - Topics
  - Communities

---

## Identity Approach

- Maintain a **consistent page identity**
- Keep **personal identity separate**
- Identity = role, not self

Example:
“This account explores AI, builds things, and documents experiments.”

---

## Validation Reframed

Not:
- “Do people like me?”

But:
- “What happens when I present this idea this way?”

Metrics are:
- **Signal**, not **verdict**

---

## Usage Constraints

### Time Containment
- Social media is only used during **Office Hours**
- No casual or continuous access

### Posting Behavior
- Post → leave → return later
- No immediate checking of reactions

### Feedback Timing
- Review responses only during next session
- Prevents emotional attachment loops

---

## Anti-Drift Rules

- Do not chase trends blindly
- Do not optimize for engagement at the cost of authenticity
- Participate in conversation without losing direction

---

## Strategy Orientation

- Long-term > short-term
- Credibility > virality
- Consistency > intensity

---

## Desired Outcome

- Build **longitudinal credibility**
- Create a **coherent body of work**
- Maintain **psychological independence**

---

## Core Identity Statement

“I publish consistently as a record of what I’m building and thinking — not as a performance for validation.”
