# Office Hours System

## Purpose
Office Hours exist as a **container for reality** — a structured space where administrative, logistical, and “should-be” domains are handled deliberately and without emotional leakage into the rest of life.

The goal is not productivity for its own sake, but **psychological closure**.

By giving these domains a defined place, they stop occupying background attention and allow for guilt-free living outside of them.

---

## Core Philosophy

- Office Hours are **containment, not control**
- They are a **pressure release valve**, not a performance arena
- They exist to **remove friction from life**, not define it
- They are **finite and bounded** (first half of the day only)

---

## Schedule

- **Days:** Monday & Tuesday
- **Duration:** First half of the day (flexible, not rigid)
- **Start Ritual:** Light yoga / grounding session

Office Hours do not override life:
- Travel, meaningful experiences, or connection take priority
- Missing a session intentionally is acceptable if aligned with life direction

---

## Categories Within Office Hours

### 1. Accounting
- Monthly cash flow tracking
- Income vs expenses
- Financial awareness (simple, not obsessive)

### 2. Finance
- Reviewing accounts
- Retirement planning
- Long-term financial positioning

### 3. Health
- Scheduling appointments (doctor, dentist)
- Preventative care planning
- Awareness of family health history
- Light dietary reflection

### 4. Environment / Office Maintenance
- Cleaning and organizing living/working space
- Treating the home as a functional workspace

### 5. Preparedness
- Scenario planning (e.g., lost phone, emergencies)
- Reducing fragility in daily systems

### 6. Structured Learning (Optional / Emerging)
- Focused, intentional learning blocks
- Only included if clearly defined and scoped

---

## Operating Rules

- Each session must include **clear, finishable targets**
- Avoid vague tasks (“look into X”)
- Prefer concrete actions:
  - Book appointment
  - Log into account and record balances
  - Complete one defined step

---

## Psychological Constraints

- Stop before burnout
- Avoid turning sessions into a “moral proving ground”
- Maintain **lightness and sustainability**

---

## Desired Outcome

- Build **trust with self**
- Reduce background anxiety
- Enable **clean, guilt-free free time**

Office Hours are successful when:
- Life outside them feels lighter
- Decisions feel less pressured
- “Should-be” thoughts decrease

---

## Relationship to Life

Office Hours support:
- Wu Wei (effortless living)
- Creative exploration
- Physical health routines
- Relationships

They do not compete with them.

---

## Core Identity Statement

“I handle what needs handling… so I can fully live when it’s time to live.”
