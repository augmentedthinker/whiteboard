## What we have actually been building

At the highest level, we have not been building a single app so much as a multi-lane human-plus-agent ecosystem. Several strands now form the current architecture.

Remote Viewer has evolved well beyond an artifact launcher. It now functions as a browser-native surface for:

- current artifacts and experiments
- identity and context documents
- continuity and re-entry pages
- strategic analysis
- capability and priority thinking
- outward-facing proof of work

This is one of the most important structural moves in the whole system. It lowers re-entry cost, gives thoughts a visual home, and makes the collaboration inspectable rather than purely conversational.

### 2. Balance as inward-facing life architecture

Balance remains one of the deepest personal lanes. But the older nine-domain symmetry stopped feeling fully alive. The new four-force direction is stronger:

- **Venture / Applied AI**
- **Connection / Relational Identity**
- **Life Stewardship**
- **Adventure / Aliveness**

This newer model feels less like a conventional balance wheel and more like a map of the major gravitational fields in Christopher’s life. That matters because it suggests a more psychologically accurate foundation for future product or reflection work.

### 3. The citation / legal lane as strongest outward-facing utility

The citation-app lane is currently the clearest external-value candidate. The judgment now seems fairly strong:

- **Balance** is the strongest inward or flagship-thesis lane.
- **The citation app** is the strongest likely first-client or first-revenue lane.

That does not mean it is magically finished. It means it is easier to explain, easier to test against reality, and closer to becoming a small legible offer.

### 4. Publishing as proof-of-work packaging

The publishing lane has become more explicit and more honest. The healthy split still appears to be:

- **X / manual social** for lightweight proof-of-work and visible public trace
- **Blogger / review-first** for deeper long-form writing

The goal is not simply more content. The goal is a publishing rhythm that documents real work without collapsing into AI slop or empty self-branding.

### 5. Continuity itself as infrastructure

There is now a real continuity discipline in place:

- daily memory notes
- long-term memory curation
- end-of-session summaries and analyses
- startup/re-entry review patterns
- Remote Viewer primers and briefing artifacts

This is not incidental overhead. It is part of the system’s actual intelligence.

## Hosting strategy

A cleaner hosting judgment is now in view:

- **GitHub Pages** remains the right default for static surfaces like Remote Viewer
- **Vercel** is likely the stronger home for more product-like apps such as the citation lane

The reason is architectural, not fashionable. Server-side key storage, serverless routes, and a cleaner tester experience would make the citation app feel more like a real product and less like a developer-facing prototype.
